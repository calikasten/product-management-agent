# **Purpose**
This skill performs one-time, in-depth market research on companies operating in the agentic commerce space. It gathers intelligence from multiple sources to create both comparative analysis and detailed individual company profiles covering company size, funding, products, product strategy, recent announcements, roadmap insights, and commercialization strategy.

## When To Use
Use this skill when the user needs to:
- Research competitors or companies in the agentic commerce industry
- Understand the competitive landscape for AI-powered product optimization, Answer Engine Optimization (AEO), AI shopping agents, or product data management for AI
- Gather intelligence on specific companies' products, strategies, and market positioning
- Create a snapshot of the market at a specific point in time

Do NOT use this skill for:
- Research outside the agentic commerce/AI commerce space
- General company research unrelated to their agentic commerce products

# **Overall Agent Process**
1. **Identify Target Companies:** Start with the user-provided list and conduct web research to discover additional companies in the agentic commerce space.
2. **Gather Company Intelligence:** For each company, research and collect data on size, funding, products, strategy, announcements, and go-to-market approach.
3. **Analyze Product Strategy:** Evaluate each company's product positioning, target market, feature prioritization, integration strategy, pricing model, and technology approach.
4. **Analyze Go-to-Market Strategy:** Assess commercialization approach including sales model, marketing channels, partnerships, and customer acquisition strategy.
5. **Create Comparison Table:** Build a structured comparison matrix showing key metrics and strategic positioning across all companies.
6. **Write Individual Profiles:** Create detailed profiles for each company with comprehensive analysis.
7. **Save Research Output:** Save the complete research document to the workspace for future reference.

## Specific Process Details

### 1. Identify Target Companies
Start with any companies the user explicitly mentions. Then use web search to discover additional companies by searching for:
- "agentic commerce platforms"
- "AI product optimization tools"
- "Answer Engine Optimization" or "AEO platforms"
- "AI shopping agent platforms"
- "product data management for AI"
- "AI-powered product discovery"
- "ChatGPT shopping optimization"
- "Perplexity commerce optimization"

Look for companies that offer software platforms, tools, or services in these areas. Exclude general e-commerce platforms unless they have specific agentic commerce capabilities.

### 2. Gather Company Intelligence
For each identified company, research and document:

**Company Size:**
- Number of employees (check LinkedIn, company website careers page, Crunchbase, news articles)
- Geographic presence and headquarters location
- Year founded

**Funding Information:**
- Total funding raised (check Crunchbase, PitchBook, TechCrunch, company press releases)
- Funding stage (Seed, Series A/B/C, etc.)
- Key investors
- Most recent funding round date and amount
- If bootstrapped or profitable, note that

**Products:**
- List all relevant products/services for agentic commerce
- Core features and capabilities of each product
- Target users (brands, retailers, agencies, etc.)
- Integration capabilities and supported platforms

Use these sources:
- Company website (product pages, about page, press/news section)
- LinkedIn company page
- Crunchbase
- TechCrunch and other tech news sites
- Product Hunt
- Company blog and documentation
- Press releases
- Industry analyst reports if available
- Customer case studies

### 3. Analyze Product Strategy
For each company, evaluate and document:

**Target Market & Positioning:**
- Primary customer segments (enterprise brands, retailers, SMBs, agencies)
- Industry verticals they focus on
- How they position themselves vs. competitors

**Feature Prioritization:**
- Core capabilities they emphasize
- Recent feature launches that indicate strategic direction
- What problems they prioritize solving

**Integration Strategy:**
- Key platforms they integrate with (e.g., Shopify, Adobe, Salesforce, PIMs)
- Approach to data ingestion and distribution
- API and ecosystem strategy

**Pricing Model:**
- Pricing structure if publicly available (subscription, usage-based, enterprise)
- Free trial or freemium offerings
- Pricing transparency level

**Technology Approach:**
- Use of AI/ML in their product
- Proprietary technology or models mentioned
- Technical differentiation claims

### 4. Analyze Go-to-Market Strategy
For each company, assess:

**Sales Model:**
- Self-service vs. sales-led
- Enterprise focus vs. SMB focus
- Partner/channel strategy

**Marketing Channels:**
- Content marketing approach (blog, resources, guides)
- Thought leadership and educational content
- Events, webinars, conferences
- Social media presence
- SEO and organic visibility

**Partnerships:**
- Technology partnerships
- Agency partnerships
- Integration partners
- Strategic alliances

**Customer Acquisition:**
- Lead generation tactics
- Free tools or assessments offered
- Demo or trial availability
- Case studies and social proof

**Market Positioning:**
- Key messaging and value propositions
- Competitive differentiation claims
- Category creation or category leadership positioning

### 5. Create Comparison Table
Build a markdown table with the following columns:
- Company Name
- Founded
- Employees
- Funding Stage
- Total Funding
- Primary Product(s)
- Target Customer
- Key Differentiator
- Pricing Model
- Notable Integrations

Format the table for easy scanning and comparison.

### 6. Write Individual Profiles
For each company, create a detailed profile with these sections:

**Company Overview**
- Brief description
- Headquarters and size
- Founding story if relevant

**Funding & Growth**
- Detailed funding history
- Investors
- Growth indicators

**Product Portfolio**
- Detailed description of each relevant product
- Key features and capabilities
- Product screenshots or demos if available

**Product Strategy Analysis**
- Assessment of their strategic approach
- Target market and positioning
- Feature prioritization patterns
- Integration and ecosystem strategy
- Technology differentiation

**Recent Announcements & Innovations**
- Product launches in the past 6-12 months
- Partnerships announced
- Funding announcements
- Awards or recognition
- Include links to press releases and news articles

**Roadmap & Future Direction**
- Any publicly stated roadmap items
- Signals about future direction from recent hires, acquisitions, or announcements
- Analyst predictions if available

**Go-to-Market & Commercialization Strategy**
- Sales approach and model
- Marketing strategy and channels
- Partnership strategy
- Customer acquisition tactics
- Market positioning and messaging
- Competitive positioning

**Sources**
- List all sources used with links

### 7. Save Research Output
Save the complete research document as a markdown file with the current date in the filename.

Structure the document with:
1. Executive Summary
2. Comparison Table
3. Individual Company Profiles (one section per company)
4. Market Insights & Trends (synthesis across companies)

# **Final Instructions**
1. Be thorough but efficient - use web search strategically to find the most relevant and recent information.
2. When information is not publicly available, note that explicitly rather than speculating.
3. Include direct links to all sources, especially press releases and announcements.
4. If you discover companies that are tangentially related but not core to agentic commerce, note them separately as "Adjacent Companies" rather than full profiles.
5. Prioritize recent information (last 12-18 months) for announcements and strategy analysis.
